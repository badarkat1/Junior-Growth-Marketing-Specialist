# Post Title / Hook
How I went from overcomplicating content to simplicity

# Full Content
I’m a chronic overthinker… I used to blame the algorithm. Really, it was: – Inconsistency. – No system. – Making things harder than they needed. When I flipped my strategy, I saw: ‘Hitting publish can't be the end goal.’ ‘Repurposing ideas, not content, is a cheat code.’ ‘Marketers who grow fastest repeat simple systems.’ Yes: Creation and promotion matter. But no, you don’t need new content every week or all platforms. I built the Distribution First Framework: Plan simply. Promote smarter. Remix consistently. The framework will help you: Plan simply. Promote smarter. Remix consistently.

# Summary: Simon shares that he simplified his approach by focusing on consistent execution and content repurposing. He discourages overthinking: a proper system means publishing, then continually promoting and remixing ideas across formats. He introduces his “Distribution First Framework” around planning, promotion, and remixing.

# Main Idea / Strategy
System > Novelty: Growth comes from consistency and a repeatable system, not from reinventing content or juggling platforms.
Repurpose Content: Use the same core ideas in multiple ways; repurposing is a “cheat code” to extend reach and reinforce messages.
Distribution Focus: Emphasize promoting content smartly (e.g., building a funnel, email list, webinars) rather than obsessing over producing new material.
