# Post 1

# Post Title / Hook
More content” usually isn’t the answer

# Full Content
“"More content" usually isn't the answer. "More clarity" is. Before you publish another blog or social update, ask: • What question am I actually answering? • Who exactly is this for? (Be specific.) • Does this genuinely make their day better, easier, or smarter? Clear beats clever every time. Cleverness comes later; clarity comes first.”

# Summary: Natividad advises that adding content volume without clear purpose misses the point. Each piece should solve a specific question for a defined audience. Clarity in messaging is prioritized over being clever or just “more.”

# Main Idea / Strategy
1. Clarity over Volume: Focus on solving a real audience question with each post, rather than increasing quantity.
2. Audience Focus: Precisely define “who” the content is for before creating it. Make it genuinely useful to their day or work.
3. Intentional Content: Ensure every piece has a clear purpose (what, who, why now) to make content meaningful (as one commenter notes, content strategy hinges on clear promise to audience).

# Post 2
2025-09

# Post Title / Hook
Everyone thinks posting on LinkedIn is hard

# Full Content
"Everyone thinks posting on LinkedIn is hard. It’s not. It’s about showing up consistently and sharing what only you can share. Your unique perspective is content gold. Stop overthinking: → Don’t chase trends. → Don’t wait for the perfect idea. → Don’t try to sound smart or polished. Start posting your take on small, ordinary things you notice at work. Repeat them in different formats: story, list, case-study, question, hot take… rinse and repeat. The secret isn’t creating more content. It’s sharing the same core insights in ways only you can. P.S. The best creators don’t have 100 ideas. They have 5 they repeat until everyone remembers.”

# Summary
Natividad urges consistency and authenticity: post regularly and focus on unique perspectives. She recommends repurposing a few key ideas in multiple formats (story, list, etc.) instead of constantly seeking new topics.

# Main Idea/Strategy
1. Authenticity & Consistency: Post regularly and speak from your unique viewpoint rather than following shiny trends.
2. Iterate on Core Ideas: Develop a handful of high-value insights and present them repeatedly (with different angles) until they stick.
3. Quality Over Novelty: Don’t overthink perfection. Action (posting and adapting existing insights) is more important than constantly inventing new ideas.
