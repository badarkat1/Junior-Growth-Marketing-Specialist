# Post Title / Hook
7 patterns... B2B founder who wins on LinkedIn

# Full Content
Over the years I've seen B2B founders break through on LinkedIn by following 7 key patterns: 1. One platform focus: They pick one channel (usually LinkedIn) and stick to it for a year. 2. High volume & reusable posts: 5+ posts/week; they replay hits with new hooks. 3. Real positions: They take informed stances (against myths, legacy vendors) with data or stories. 4. Content ecosystem: Founders + team + company posts create omnipresence. 5. Founder–market fit: Content latches on lived experience (the founder has done the work). 6. Tie to owned channels: Drive LinkedIn traffic to newsletter/website/CRM. 7. Support system: Often an editor or team helps execute the high cadence. Compressed playbook: 5 posts/week, one platform, strong opinions, ecosystem, matched to experience, linked to newsletter, commit to a support model.

# Summary: Leatherman lists seven success factors for founders on LinkedIn: deep commitment to one channel, frequent posting (with reusing top ideas), clear opinions, building a team content presence, leveraging founder experience, converting LinkedIn into owned leads (newsletter), and getting help to maintain quality/cadence.

# Main Idea / Strategy
One Platform, High Cadence: Prioritize LinkedIn for 12+ months with ≥5 posts/week, repurposing your top posts.
Strong POV + Team Effort: Take smart, data-backed stances; involve teammates and company posts to amplify reach (founder + team = omnipresence).
Content to Pipeline: Link posts to owned media (newsletter, email list) to turn engagement into leads. Consistent systems (support or DIY) are essential.

# Post Title / Hook
Founder-led content is your insurance policy

# Full Content
Founder-led content is not vanity; it's a risk management tool. When a quarter goes sideways (lost clients, slipped deals), founders who have been posting for years don’t panic—they activate a warm audience who already trust them. They have: – A list of “warm but not yet” prospects following their content. – A newsletter audience receiving weekly value. – Past customers and peers to reach out for referrals. – Buyers who know their POV from reading their posts. Content is insurance: you notice its value when things go wrong. Too often, founders wait for a crisis. By then, it's 12 months too late. Build the asset now (5 posts/week on LinkedIn, newsletter, and a warm list in CRM) so when your pipeline tightens you have trust to pull on.

# Summary
Leatherman emphasizes consistent, founder-led LinkedIn publishing as a form of insurance: it builds a ready audience that can be activated when sales falter. He advises posting regularly and capturing emails so that when deals slip, you have trust and channels to bounce back.

# Main Idea/Strategy
Content as Insurance: Regular content accrual protects against downturns by creating a network that trusts you.
Long-term Consistency: Ideal routine: LinkedIn 5×/week + weekly newsletter + CRM tagging of prospects; doing it before a crisis is key.
Compound Value: Even without immediate pipeline, steady audience-building yields leads over time—trust built preemptively pays off in future quarters.
