# Post Title / Hook
Why marketing agencies often fail to deliver real strategy

# Full Content
Marketing agencies often drive me nuts: they walk in a meeting and immediately pitch channels (‘You should be on YouTube, LinkedIn, try a podcast!’). But they’re selling tools, not strategy. They use standard playbooks without understanding the business. Real strategy is connecting the dots: e.g. Google ads get leads, but sales follow through on calls, Instagram gave visibility but low conversions… Rather than say ‘LinkedIn engagement is low’, insight is ‘use LinkedIn for precise targeting, but drive conversions via personalized email sequences.’ Strategy isn’t picking channels off a menu; it’s understanding how they work together for your goals.

# Summary: Kaura argues that agencies too often recommend platforms by habit instead of diagnosing business needs. He stresses that strategy involves analyzing outcomes (e.g. engagement vs conversion) and advising adjustments (e.g. shift budget, coordinate channels) rather than vanity metrics. Agencies should deliver actionable insights, not just reports.

# Main Idea / Strategy
System Thinking: Marketing success is about how channels (LinkedIn, email, PPC) integrate to drive results, not isolated tactics.
Data-Driven Recommendations: Move beyond raw metrics (“LinkedIn likes went up”) to explain performance (“LinkedIn drives leads, but need email nurture to convert”); that’s real strategy.
Insight vs. Reporting: Agencies should provide insights (what to change and why) instead of just metrics. Ask “how does each platform serve our funnel?” rather than “should we be on X?”.
